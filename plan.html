Assignment 2 - Barrier Reef Orchestra website

Mission Statement

The purpose of the Barrier Reef Orchestra (BRO) website is to increase revenue for the orchestra by promoting upcoming performances with a view to attracting a new base of concert goers from a younger demographic. Advertising the benefits of membership (both community and musician membership) to the younger demographic is a secondary priority for site as it is the primary method for attracting new comers into the Orchestra.

Success Evaluation

Analyse the demographics of the current membership and survey the next concert performance to create base-line reporting; Specifically counting the ratio of 15-30-year-old compared to 35-50 and over 50-year-old age groups. Then after a 12-month period following the release of the new website, repeat the membership analysis and survey to check if there has been an increase in the younger demographic members and concert goers. All other advertising including banners placed around various physical sites throughout the local community and social media (Facebook) is to remain consistent during this period so any increase in younger demographic can be attributed to the website.

Prior to the website being launch financial figures will be obtained to determine funds received from memberships, sponsorships and donations. After the website is launched the reports will continue to be run on a quarterly basis to determine if funds received by the Orchestra have increased or decreased as a result of the website. If the website is successful the Orchestra will see a noticable increase in memberships, sponsorships and donations received, helping the to acheive the target of increased revenue.

Target Audience

The BRO website is aimed at a younger target audience of 15-30-year-old. The look and feel of the website will be contemporary and fresh providing for quick/high-level navigation so the end user can find the information they are seeking in a quick and concise manner to match the busy schedule that this demographic tends to have. The website will contain little text-based content at the higher levels rather using visual tools to help the user scan the content such as calls for action and linked images that draw the users attention. This will assist concert goers seeking a brief interaction with the site to obtain the information they are chasing. However detailed information will still be maintained where relevant as a means of conveying the prestige of the BRO but these will generally be multiple clicks into the site only to be read by those users interested in finding out further information.

Content

  • index page (including announcements, advertising to upcoming concerts, call for community members to join, call for musicians to join, 1 x artist profile (i.e. upcoming special guest artist or composer), benefactors and sponsors section in footer
  • separate page for each upcoming concert (Beethoven 19 August, Evening Serenade 28 October) (list key information for each event i.e. date, venue, ticket cost, where to buy tickets, special guests artist/composer profile, programme for the event etc)
  • concerts page (clearly divided into upcoming and past concerts - upcoming to link to the above pages. Past events serve as an archive of past events)
  • artists page (profiles/feature notes on past special guest artists and conductors (upcoming artists to be profiles on the event page)
  • membership page (benefits of membership (both community and concert goer membership), link to membership form, list of life members and brief description of their contribution to club)
  • benefactor page (re-list the current benefactors including a "since date" column to encourage repeat donations, benefits of donating (free tickets, name listed on site), how to donate (link to download form), list of items where the money will be spent)
  • about us (includes: mission statement of Orchestra ("To bring music to all members of the community wanting to find out about music"- Judy Hunter 2016), history of BRO, photo gallery, links section (links to interesting pages and YouTube videos i.e. pages that provide information about orchestras, You Tube videos mentioned in client briefing, other events around town, other orchestras etc).
  • buy tickets (link straight to PayPal or Civic Theatre ticket site)
  • contact us (contact details, Facebook details, committee details)
